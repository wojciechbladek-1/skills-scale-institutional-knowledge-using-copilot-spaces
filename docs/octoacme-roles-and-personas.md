# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Masters

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate delivery rhythms and continuous improvement in Agile teams. They help teams remove blockers and keep collaboration healthy.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate ceremonies (standups, planning, review, retrospective)
- Remove delivery impediments and escalate blockers when needed
- Coach the team on agreed process and working agreements
- Support transparent progress tracking and predictable cadence

### Goals
- Maintain sustainable delivery flow
- Reduce blockers and handoff delays
- Improve team effectiveness over time

### Typical Communication
- Daily facilitation in standups
- Weekly sync with Project Manager on blockers and risks
- Retrospective facilitation and follow-up actions

### Interaction with Other Roles
- Partners with **Project Manager** on delivery cadence and escalations
- Supports **Product Managers** in refinement and sprint goal clarity
- Helps **Developers** keep work in progress manageable
- Aligns with **QA Lead** on test readiness in sprint flow

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts translate business needs into clear, testable requirements and help ensure shared understanding across stakeholders.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit and document requirements and assumptions
- Clarify acceptance criteria with Product Manager and Developers
- Trace requirements to delivered scope and outcomes
- Highlight requirement gaps, dependencies, and policy constraints

### Goals
- Reduce ambiguity in scope and priorities
- Improve requirement quality before implementation
- Ensure delivered work maps to business outcomes

### Typical Communication
- Requirement workshops with stakeholders
- Backlog refinement sessions with Product and Engineering
- Decision logs for scope and requirement changes

### Interaction with Other Roles
- Works with **Product Managers** to shape backlog-ready items
- Supports **Project Managers** by clarifying dependencies and scope changes
- Collaborates with **Developers** and **QA Lead** on testable acceptance criteria
- Partners with **UX Designers** to align user needs and business goals

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers ensure solutions are usable, accessible, and aligned with user needs through design exploration and validation.

### Responsibilities
- Create and iterate user flows, wireframes, and prototypes
- Define UX acceptance criteria and interaction expectations
- Run lightweight user validation and synthesize feedback
- Collaborate with engineering on feasible, consistent implementation

### Goals
- Improve usability and adoption
- Reduce rework caused by unclear interaction design
- Maintain consistent product experience

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product and Engineering
- Asynchronous feedback in design artifacts and tickets
- Usability findings shared with stakeholders

### Interaction with Other Roles
- Aligns with **Product Managers** on user outcomes and priorities
- Works with **Business Analysts** on requirement clarity
- Collaborates with **Developers** and **Tech Lead / Engineering Manager** on implementation trade-offs
- Partners with **QA Lead** on UX-focused acceptance checks

---

## Tech Leads / Engineering Managers

### Role Summary
Tech Leads / Engineering Managers guide technical direction, engineering quality, and team execution alignment.

### Responsibilities
- Define technical approach, architecture guardrails, and standards
- Support estimation, sequencing, and dependency management
- Review technical risk and ensure operational readiness
- Coach developers and coordinate cross-team engineering decisions

### Goals
- Deliver maintainable, scalable systems
- Balance speed with reliability and technical health
- Reduce technical uncertainty early

### Typical Communication
- Technical design discussions and review comments
- Weekly engineering risk/dependency sync with PM and Product
- Incident and post-incident technical updates

### Interaction with Other Roles
- Partners with **Project Managers** on scope, sequencing, and risk
- Works with **Product Managers** to align trade-offs and feasibility
- Supports **Developers** through code/design review and mentorship
- Aligns with **QA Lead** and **Release Manager** on release quality and readiness

---

## QA Leads

### Role Summary
QA Leads coordinate the project quality strategy across test planning, execution, and release confidence.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain test strategy for critical flows
- Coordinate manual and automated testing coverage
- Track defects, quality risks, and release quality signals
- Confirm acceptance and regression readiness before release

### Goals
- Prevent critical defects from reaching production
- Improve confidence in each release
- Reduce quality-related rework and incident volume

### Typical Communication
- Test plan and quality status updates in sprint/release forums
- Defect triage with Developers and Product
- Go/no-go quality recommendations for releases

### Interaction with Other Roles
- Works with **Developers** on testability and defect resolution
- Partners with **Project Manager** and **Scrum Master** on quality-related blockers
- Aligns with **Product Managers** and **Business Analysts** on acceptance criteria
- Coordinates with **Release Manager** during release readiness and deployment windows

---

## Release Managers

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate release readiness, deployment execution, and stakeholder communication across release events.

### Responsibilities
- Plan release windows, sequencing, and deployment communications
- Verify readiness criteria and coordinate go/no-go checkpoints
- Coordinate rollback plans and incident communication paths
- Capture release outcomes and follow-up actions

### Goals
- Deliver predictable, low-risk releases
- Minimize release coordination gaps
- Improve release transparency for stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Release readiness reviews and deployment announcements
- Cross-team coordination with engineering, QA, and support
- Post-release summaries and issue follow-up

### Interaction with Other Roles
- Partners with **QA Lead** on release quality gate signals
- Works with **Project Manager** on timeline and stakeholder coordination
- Aligns with **Tech Lead / Engineering Manager** and **Developers** on deployment feasibility and rollback readiness
- Coordinates with **Product Managers** on release scope and stakeholder messaging
